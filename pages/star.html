A stoic ruler figure is depicted on the throne of the Emperor tarot card, which features four rams' heads as symbols for the ruler's astrological sign of Aries. He holds an orb, a representation of the realm he guards, in one hand and a sceptre, which stand for his authority and dominion, in the other. The emperor's long beard symbolises his extensive experience; through the years, he has gained a great deal of knowledge about what it takes to lead, to establish power, authority, and total order for the good of his people.

Behind him, the barren mountains shows his determination, his ambition for greater heights and his leadership capability. Contrast this with his complement, the Empress, whose flowing fields are filled with nurturing kindness. The Emperor instead rules with grit, strength and force.

Meeting him in a reading can also be interpreted as a call to the adventurous side of your own personality. He gives people courage because he recognises that every day presents an opportunity to explore new possibilities in life, which brings with it a mix of anticipation, wonder, awe, and curiosity. The Fool serves as a reminder that you can never truly know what lies ahead, therefore you should always approach it with fun.

"The Fool is the first card in the deck, but it’s not number one: it’s zero. It’s nothing rather than something: the vast, open emptiness from which everything arises and into which it dissolves."
